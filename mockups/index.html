🧠

ADHD-supportive by design

New · visible in all 3 mockups · grounded in cognitive offloading research

Every mockup embodies one principle: inform, don't decide. We surface the information your brain wants to see — trade-offs, density, travel time, what's already in your week — and leave the judgment to you. No auto-decline. No streak shame. No "we recommend."

📊
Week at a glance

A 7-day density strip shows what's already on your calendar. Your visual cortex does the math — no score, no judgment.

Externalizes working memory

💡
Worth noticing chips

Neutral chips like "12-min walk", "first open Sat", "CSCI 5801 is recorded". No chip ever says "don't go."

Decision scaffolding, not direction

🕒
Leave by clock

Concrete "leave by 4:48 PM" directly targets time blindness. One tap to add. No nagging, no reminders.

Targets the task-initiation wall

Anti-patterns we refuse to ship: auto-declining events, streak shame, red-badge nagging, "we recommend," infantilizing copy, gamified guilt.

Grounded in Risko & Gilbert 2016 (cognitive offloading), the Ottawa Decision Support Framework, Google PAIR's explainability guidance, and pattern analysis of Goblin Tools, Tiimo, and Sunsama.
